Strategic Assessment

Action Required
⚠ Extreme Concentration Risk

Government Procurement Services Agency (Acct 61000) alone holds TZS 37B — 49% of total net outstanding. The top 3 accounts represent over 85%. A single payment delay or dispute at GPSA would critically impair liquidity and debt service capacity.

⚠ Chronic Long-Aged Debt — IFRS 9 Exposure

TZS 35.5B (47%) is over 1 year old. TZS 2.3B+ dates back more than 3 years across 7 accounts exceeding TZS 1B each. Under IFRS 9 Expected Credit Loss (ECL) model, significant impairment provisions are required and will impact FY2026 earnings.

▲ Below-Benchmark Collection Efficiency

At 32.3%, collection performance is less than half the automotive sector benchmark of 65–80%. This implies TZS 30–40B in recoverable receivables that require structured recovery programs, dedicated account managers, and escalation protocols.

▲ Government Sector Concentration

Ministries (Defence, Health, Minerals, President's Office) and state agencies (TRA, GPSA, National Service) account for a disproportionate share of long-aged debt. Government debtors require formal demand letters, ministerial engagement, and Treasury offset mechanisms.

ℹ Credit Balance Liability — TZS 27B

TZS 27.0B held across 2,141 accounts in credit may include unprocessed payments, pricing adjustments, or returned goods. Bulyanhulu Gold (TZS 1.75B) and Swala Solutions (TZS 1.16B) are the largest. Reconciliation and netting should precede any refunds.

✓ Mining Sector — Recovery Opportunity

Geita Gold Mining (2 entities), North Mara Gold Mine, and several Chinese civil construction firms show high invoice volumes with genuine commercial activity. Structured payment plans and letters of credit could unlock TZS 3–5B in recoveries within 90 days.
